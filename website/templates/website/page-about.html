The Founder

  • The Sleevie Wonder was dreamt up by Victoria Blow while working in a "hip" barber shop in Los Angeles. She noticed that the professionals who would come in on their lunch hour or after work would be wearing nice, long sleeved shirts and also have business to attend to (on their smartphones).
  • Realizing that the clients in their dressy work clothes would prefer to be able to continue about their day without hair all over their sleeves, she thought "There must be a simple solution!" So the Sleevie Wonder was born, sewn by Victoria's Mom on a 40 year old Singer Sewing machine and using a salad plate as a neck hole template.
  • As the development progressed, Victoria noticed that clients can also benefit from having sleeves when they are reading or enjoying a beverage while they are in the chair. She made the sleeves extra long and the openings wide to accommodate a tall beverage to be held "shrugged" into the sleeve.
